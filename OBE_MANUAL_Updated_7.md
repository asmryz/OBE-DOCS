**Course Objectives**  
| | |  
|-|-|  
|By the completion of this course, the student should be able to gain a thorough understanding of the basic concepts of Calculus of one variable. This includes functions, limits, continuity, derivatives of functions and their applications, the definition of the integral, the Fundamental Theorem of Calculus, some applications of integration, series convergence, Taylor and Maclaurin series, power series.

   
**Course Description**  
| | |  
|-|-|  
|This course begins with a review of function; then the idea of limit and continuity is introduced. With the knowledge of limits and continuity, the student develops the concept of the derivative and its applications. Further, the student studies the anti-derivative of elementary functions and the applications of the definite integral in geometry, science, and engineering. At the end, series convergence is introduced.

is designed for beginners with little or no prior programming experience. The course covers problem-
solving techniques, algorithm development, and structured programming concepts. Students will learn
the syntax and semantics of C, including variables, data types, operators, control structures, functions,
arrays, pointers, strings, and file handling. Emphasis is placed on writing efficient, well-structured,
and error-free code through hands-on practice and programming assignments. By the end of the course,
students will be able to design, implement, debug, and document C programs to solve basic
computational problems, providing a solid foundation for advanced courses in programming and
computer science.
TextBook
